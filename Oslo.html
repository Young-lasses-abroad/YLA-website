Oslo is one of Scandanavia's most expensive cities but also a gorgeous city that is situated along the Oslofjords.

To narrow it down for you here's our lasses' Top 3...


Akershus Fortress

The Akershus Fortress is a medieval castle that was built for the royals in the 1290s.
Since then, the fortress has been used as a prison and military base. The achitecture
also provided inspiration for the Disney film Frozen which was also set in Norway.


Opera House

Oslo Opera house showcases ballet and opera and is a must see when visiting Norway.
The building itself also shows spectacular sites at the front of the fjords.


Oslo Salt

The Oslo SALT is a hub for creative individuals to immerse themselves in art, music,
food and architecture. Overlooking the Oslofjords are the prymaid wooden pannels
and inside the building is a communal sauna area. To recieve the full experience of
the sauna, those daring enough jump into the freezing cold fjord waters, climb back
out, go back into the sauna and repeat.

We know keeping yourself safe is a top priority when galavanting abroad but have no fear gals, there are plenty of options. details about particular city and what they have to offer

Heres our top 3 recommended ways to get around

1. Metro

Oslo is well connected with their metro connections, running a fast and reliable
service this is the optimum way to travel round.


2. Bus

To live like a full local, travel via bus. As with all cities, this can sometimes
be the best way to see the sights and is the cheaper method of transport


3. VOI Scooters

Like all Scandanavian countries, Norway has a superb cycling culture. But even better
than cycling is the electric scooters which can be found all around the capital.
I'd reccomend the VOI company as I found it really easy to find scooters with their
very efficient app.
