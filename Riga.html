🏙️First things first, whats there do to!?

In Riga theres plenty of things to see and do from the old town & beautiful gardens.

To narrow it down for you heres our lasses top 3

Nativity Cathedral

A Orthodox Church with Stunning architecture is a place not to miss in Riga.
It also comprises part of Riga’s unique history from the soviet era
It’s free to enter- but be nice & pop some cash in the donation box.

Town Hall Square

Contains some stunning architecture and history that spans from medieval to current day.
House of the blackheads (yes that’s it’s real name😂) is the largest monument in the square.
Be sure to come between Tuesday-Sunday in order to look inside the house. And
contrasting to the bright House of Blackheads, is a small dark Soviet Building that now
contains a museum. The Roland Statue within the square depicts Lativa’s medieval
privileges.

Lazienki Park

It is Warsaw’s largest park, with the most striking beauty. Escape the stress of city life
and enjoy finding your ‘zen’ here. Lazienki competes favorably with other top parks in the
world. From its central lake and stately residences to an amphitheater and space wide enough
to host popular concerts, Lazienki has plenty to offer. You might even come across elegant
peacocks as you wander through the park. Even on crowded days, finding a quiet spot is easy.

We know keeping yourself safe is a top priority when galavanting abroad but have no fear gals, there are plenty of options. details about particular city and what they have to offer

Heres our top 3 recommended ways to get around

1. Electric Bikes


There’s a few options but my personal favourite is ‘Bolt’ It has an easy smartphone app for
unlocking/locking the bikes. It doesn’t come with a helmet- so bring or buy one. I prefer
riding round on a scooter to public transport- as you can just stop when you see something.

2. On Foot


As nearly a 1/5 of Riga is parks, riga is probably best explored on foot. It’s mostly flat terrain,
and it’s not a small city but it’s very compact. Riga is particularly safe, but still take
precautions at night!

3. Tram/Metro
